Finding the Man of Your Dreams

TrueData's core product is matching our customers with the perfect audiences for them. For a long time, this was a manual process, performed by our wonderful Customer Management team and Sales. Our team would process the results and email them to our customers. As our client list grew, the workload was not scalable for our Customer Management team and we decided to take our internal tool and make it customer facing.

Over the next year and a half we would take this tool from a bare forum to a unique tool that solved pain points in ways none of our competitors were.

The initial tools available on our audience builder were:

  • 1. Audience name input
  • 2. List of apps your customers would ideally have installed on their mobile device
  • 3. List of apps your customers would ideally have not installed on their mobile device
  • 4. Age range of targeted audience (check boxes)
  • 5. Gender of Target Audience (only M/F options)
  • 6. Device Characteristics (Phone manufacturer, and Model)

We wanted to get the tooling in our customers hands as quickly as possible, and began testing with them immediately. Talking to 5 customers, I performed qualitative research to learn more about what was needed in the product, what was desired, what our competitors did right, and where our competitors had failed.

Our initial thought process was to improve Step 5 in the customers journey, by giving our competitors more precise selections than what our competitors were giving them. This would improve our target metric of outperforming our competitors, and lower our customers cost per click. Additionally we had no way of visualizing for the users the total size of the Audience was. Finally, our aesthetics needed updating, so I built our companies first design system to apply to all designs moving forward.

I started by adding specificity to the Audience Builder. For each selection I included logistical operators such as "and"/"or". I knew not all marketers come from a tech background, and included tooltip hovers and contextual visuals.

[Pic]

Next, we added an "Audience Scale Meter" to not only show the size of the selected audience, but to Give context to our Marketing customers about what the size of the selected audience means. For example, if the audience is too large, the benefits of targeting becomes nearly useless. If the audience becomes too small, your campaign wont have enough reach (If it gets TOO small, you're invading privacy, and I set up safeguards to prevent individual privacy being invaded).

I presented these designs back to our engineers and our customers and wrote a research document to clarify my findings. The general feedback was:

Feedback
The primary benefit of the logistical operators was simply revealing how the audience was being concatenated together.

Action
For version one, remove ability to toggle between operators, our backend wasn't designed to support this functionality and would take up too many engineering resources early on. After we built the toggles, a Fullstory analysis proved this point by showing just how rarely people tried to toggle.

Feedback
What our customers really loved about our platform was their interactions with he customer service team. Our Customer Service team was building our customers audiences; helping discover new audiences, and test out multiple audiences at once.

Action
Make our audience builder more flexible, reimagine the way our customers interact with our platform. Allow customers to build and submit multiple audiences at once. Add audience plans to groups called "test plans"

Looking into the future

So with pre-launch feedback in mind, we launched to a small set of customers to validate the feedback, see what was working, and to start freeing up our Customer Service team's calendars.

Our product was succeeding! We had a product that customers wanted, and was performing better than our competitors. But clearly it could be improved. We were marketing our tool as a platform to build audiences quickly and cheaply. So why were customers only building 2 per month? Thinking back to the feedback we got, We started a year long process about re-engineering our designs and our backend to start grouping audiences into "Test plans", and allow users to build multiple audiences at once. I also started working with our Data Scientist to develop a way to suggest audiences to customers using Machine Learning.

My first solution was to add a "Quick builder". Most of the audiences being built were focused on what apps the ideal customer used. I designed and tested a modal to go in our main toolbar that allowed our customers to see overlap of customers for any two companies, and select between the overlap or the select audience.

Finally, I started the process of implementing our new audience selection tool. We kept the manual forum, for customers who wanted precise control; but the new suggested audience builder quickly became the favorite, and most used tool on the platform. Customers who used the new audience selection tool built 10x the number of audiences per month as customers who purely used the manual builder, and had a 50% lower CPC.

Conclusion

This project was a healthy reminder to not just learn what the product can do better, but whatit has the potential to be. Your customers are coming to you because your competitors aren't filling their needs properly, so be bold, authentic, and standout from the rest.
